Support Playlists: Emotional navigation for real life

You say how you feel — by voice or text.
You get a Playlist: 3–6 small, doable steps.
Grounded in emotional truth. Low effort. Low pressure. Non-clinical.

A Playlist isn't therapy or advice. It's navigation — a gentle shift out of stuckness.

"I feel like I'm letting everyone down"

1. Name it:

"You're carrying too much care"

2. Do this:

Let one small thing go undone today — on purpose

3. Then this:

Sit for 2 minutes without fixing anything

4. Notice:

Did the weight shift at all?

Emotional Architecture:
Designing for how people actually feel

Emotional Architecture is a design discipline for building tools, products, and systems around emotional states — not business logic or clinical frameworks.

  • Psychological safety
  • Friction reduction
  • Clarity and tone
  • The feeling of being understood
  • Helping people move through emotions, not suppress them

Support Playlists is the purest expression of Emotional Architecture so far.

What if we could see the emotional climate?

Support Playlists doesn't track individuals. No profiles. No accounts. No surveillance.

Instead, each Playlist interaction creates a tiny anonymous signal — a moment of need, not a person.

Aggregated, these moments reveal:

  • The emotional climate of communities
  • Patterns of micro-struggle before they become crises
  • Early signals of overwhelm
  • What people need before they hit services

This becomes a new form of public intelligence. Not for enforcement. Not for diagnosis. For understanding.

An evolving map of mood and need, drawn only from anonymous patterns in how people seek help.
A climate map of care.
